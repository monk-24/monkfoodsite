## PART 1 — Google Search Console (do this first)

This tells Google your site exists, lets you submit your sitemap, and is where sitelinks eligibility is built.

1. Go to **https://search.google.com/search-console** and sign in with your Google account (monkfoodanalystic@gmail.com).
2. Click **Add property** → choose **URL prefix** → enter `https://monkfood.co.uk` → Continue.
3. **Verify ownership.** Easiest for GitHub Pages: pick the **HTML tag** method. Google gives you a line like `<meta name="google-site-verification" content="XXXX">`.
   - Copy that tag and send it to me — I'll paste it into your site's `<head>` and you push. Then click **Verify**.
   - (Alternative: if you own the domain via a registrar, use the **Domain** method + a DNS TXT record — more robust, covers www + non-www.)
4. Once verified, go to **Sitemaps** (left menu) → enter `sitemap.xml` → **Submit**.
5. Go to **URL Inspection** (top bar), paste `https://monkfood.co.uk/`, press Enter → **Request indexing**. Repeat for:
   - https://monkfood.co.uk/online-ordering
   - https://monkfood.co.uk/epos-solutions
   - https://monkfood.co.uk/qr-code-ordering
6. Done. Check back in 1–2 weeks under **Pages** to see what's indexed. Sitelinks (the sub-links under your result) appear automatically over time once you have brand traffic — you can't submit them.

**Tip:** In Search Console you can later **demote** a sitelink you don't want, but you can't add one.
